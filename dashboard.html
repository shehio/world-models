Two tracks, one question

Can a 3M-param chess net trained on a laptop play meaningful chess? The two tracks below test that with different sources of training signal. The pure-AZ track answers "from scratch, via self-play"; the distillation track answers "from scratch, but with a Stockfish teacher."

Track A · self-play (faithful AZ)

02 — AlphaZero from scratch

One network. Self-play games with MCTS. The visit counts at the root become a better policy than the net's prior → train the net to imitate them. Repeat. No external chess knowledge.

Five runs in this track (v2 → v5). Each fixed a flaw in the previous. Plateau at ~+372 vs random ≈ real Elo ~400–600

Track B · distillation (not AZ)

02b / 02c — supervised from Stockfish

Same network architecture, but trained by imitation of Stockfish's move choices. No self-play, no RL. The ceiling is the teacher's strength (~teacher Elo − search contribution).

Three runs (d6, d10, 02c-pending). d10 reaches real Elo ~1185 at zero AWS cost.

One number reality-check

Random ≈ Elo −1000 to −800 on any real chess rating system. So v5's "+372 vs random" maps to roughly real-world Elo 400–600 — well below a 1200-rated club player. The 02b-d10 number ("real Elo ~1185") is anchored against an actual chess engine and survives the conversion.

This is the single most important framing point in this whole journey: "+372 Elo" sounds impressive and is not. "19 wins of 100 vs SF1320" is the comparison that actually meant something — and it came from the distillation track, not the AZ-faithful track.

What we actually learned

About from-scratch AlphaZero at laptop scale

  • It does learn — every metric trended up, every run.
  • It plateaus far below any real chess engine. The paper used 5,000 TPUs; we used ~50 laptop-hours. The gap is ~10⁵.
  • The most underrated knob is train-steps : games ratio (v3b). Most "model is at capacity" plateaus are actually replay-buffer staleness.
  • Score-vs-random saturates around 0.95. After that, h2h tournaments are the only signal worth trusting.

About distillation

  • Distillation gives ~order-of-magnitude better Elo per compute-dollar than from-scratch RL at this scale.
  • The student's ceiling is roughly teacher_Elo − search_contribution. d10 (~2200) → student ~1185. The ~1000-Elo gap is the search Stockfish does that the student can't replicate in one forward pass.
  • Hard one-hot targets discard most of the teacher's knowledge. Multipv soft targets (02c) is the next step before bigger-teacher / more-data.
  • This is what Lc0 actually did: distill first, then self-play fine-tune on top.
