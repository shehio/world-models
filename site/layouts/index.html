AlphaZero used 5,000 TPUs for self-play data generation and 64 TPU-v2s to train. We use the same network (a 20-block × 256-channel ResNet, ~24M parameters) on one GPU, supervised distillation from Stockfish on 30M positions, evaluated against a calibrated 1,350-Elo opponent. Total cost: ~16 GPU-hours on a single L40S. Roughly four orders of magnitude less compute than the AlphaZero paper.

How It Works

One supervised pipeline + one MCTS at inference. The teacher is Stockfish; the student is AlphaZero's network architecture; the search at play-time is straight AlphaZero MCTS.

  1. Stockfish self-plays games at search-depth 15. At every position, before the chosen move is played, Stockfish reports its top-8 candidate moves with centipawn scores.
  2. Those scores become a soft distribution π over the 8 candidates (softmax at T = 1 pawn). The game outcome z ∈ {−1, 0, +1} is recorded from each side-to-move POV.
  3. The student trains on (state, π, z) triples — cross-entropy on policy, MSE on value. No self-play, no MCTS at training time.

The trained network is used as a prior over moves and a value estimator for leaf nodes; the decision comes from Monte Carlo Tree Search expanding the most promising lines. Routine eval uses 800 sims/move (AlphaZero's training-time value); deeper search at inference buys real Elo.

What's Next

The largest remaining gap to AlphaZero is that AZ has no teacher — its targets come from its own MCTS visit counts, not from Stockfish. The Lc0 recipe — distill first, RL second — runs an AlphaZero-style self-play loop on top of the distilled prior.

A first attempt regressed (LR was 100× too high for fine-tuning a pretrained net); a fix is in flight. In parallel, generating another 5M d15 positions to put the stronger-teacher hypothesis on equal data footing as the d10 result.
