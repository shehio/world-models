AlphaZero used 5,000 TPUs for self-play data generation and 64 TPU-v2s to train. We use the same network (a 20-block × 256-channel ResNet, ~24M parameters) on one GPU. The number above is the strongest checkpoint so far — supervised distillation from Stockfish on 30M positions, evaluated against a calibrated 1,350-Elo opponent.

Against a tighter UCI=1,800 opponent (calibrated near our model strength), the same checkpoint scores 2,004 Elo at 800 sims/move and 2,084 Elo at 4,000 sims/move — read those as the most honest numbers. Total training cost: about 16 GPU-hours on a single L40S. Roughly four orders of magnitude less compute than the AlphaZero paper.

network capacity hypothesis rejected

Doubling network depth from 20 → 40 blocks didn't help

Hypothesis: the 1,810-Elo ceiling is a representation limit. A bigger ResNet should land higher.
20-block (baseline)1,810~24M params · 5M positions
40-block (2× depth)1,810~48M params · same data
delta±0no benefit from capacity
{{ partial "capacity-chart-svg.html" . }}

We doubled the ResNet from 20 blocks (~24M params) to 40 blocks (~48M params), kept everything else identical, and trained for the same 20 epochs on the same 5M positions. The deeper network landed at the same 1,810 Elo against the UCI=1,800 anchor.

Interpretation: with 5M positions of teacher signal, 24M parameters is already over-parameterized. More capacity doesn't find structure that isn't there. Top-1 accuracy was 0.34 with the smaller net and stayed 0.34 with the bigger one — the student is hitting a teacher-signal limit, not a representation limit.

eval-side search hypothesis confirmed

{{ partial "search-curve-svg.html" . }}

The routine evaluation uses 800 MCTS sims/move (AlphaZero's training-time value). We re-ran one checkpoint at 4,000 sims against the same UCI=1,350 opponent and the same weights scored 2,084 Elo — sweeping 101 of 104 games (3 draws, 0 losses).

Interpretation: distilled priors transfer well to deeper inference-time search — exactly Lc0's empirical finding. The network's policy prior guides MCTS toward strong moves; more rollouts refine that prior into a sharper visit distribution. The "1,800 ceiling" reported from routine evals is partly an eval artifact, not a model limit.

Hypothesis: the bottleneck on the original baseline isn't network or teacher — it's data. The same recipe with 6× more positions should jump.

{{ partial "data-scale-chart-svg.html" . }}

The baseline used a 5M-position subsample of an available 30M-position dataset. We trained on the full 30M (same 20-block network, same hyperparameters, even a weaker teacher: Stockfish depth 10 vs depth 15) and the checkpoint at epoch 10 scores 2,009 Elo at UCI=1,800 — +199 over the baseline. Epoch 5 hits 2,084 Elo at UCI=1,350 (101 W / 3 D / 0 L).

The bottleneck on the original baseline was data, not teacher quality or network capacity. 16 h on a g6e.8xlarge, ~$50 of compute. The most cost-effective single change in the project. Currently generating another 5M d15 positions to test whether the stronger teacher pays off once data isn't starved.

how the network plays

At inference time, the trained network is used as a prior over moves and a value estimator for leaf nodes. The actual decision comes from Monte Carlo Tree Search, which expands the most promising lines and reports the visit-count distribution.

{{ partial "mcts-svg.html" . }}

A handful of root-level candidate moves; each sampled deeper based on the network's prior weighted against exploration. After N rollouts the chosen move is whichever was visited most. → how the method works in detail

what's next

The largest remaining gap to AlphaZero is that AZ has no teacher — its training targets come from its own MCTS visit counts, not from Stockfish. The Lc0 recipe — distill first, RL second — runs an AlphaZero-style self-play loop on top of the distilled prior. A first attempt regressed (LR was 100× too high for fine-tuning a pretrained net); a fix is in flight.

In parallel, generating another 5M d15 positions to put the stronger-teacher hypothesis on equal data footing as the d10 result.
